Resource Management

Recovery Systems and Process Efficiency

Steam Recovery

The PA oxidation reaction is exothermic — it generates heat as a byproduct of chemistry. Waste heat boilers capture this heat to produce high-pressure steam (40–50 barg), which drives captive turbine generators producing 52 million kWh per year.

This is why 96% of our site power is self-generated. The marginal cost is effectively zero — we are recovering energy from a reaction that must happen regardless.

Process Yield Optimization

The theoretical maximum selectivity for o-xylene to PA is approximately 94% (1.08–1.15 kg PA per kg OX). Our DCS-controlled reactors across all five plants continuously optimise temperature, air-to-o-xylene ratio, and catalyst bed conditions to operate as close to this limit as the catalyst lifecycle permits.

Higher selectivity means more product from the same feedstock — and less material entering waste streams. This is measured batch-by-batch and reported in our energy balance.

Byproduct Utilization

O-xylene oxidation produces PA as the primary product (70–80% selectivity), but also generates Maleic Anhydride (4–6%), distillation residues, and other streams. Rather than treating these as waste, IGPL recovers them commercially: 7,660 MTPA of MA, 1,300 MTPA of BA, and 8,400 MTPA of DEP.

Additionally, 3,445 MT/yr of distillation bottoms residue (DBE) is burned as fuel in thermal fluid heaters — replacing external fuel oil. Waste-to-fuel, built into the chemistry.

Safety & Compliance

Safety as an Operating Discipline

IGPL operates five plants handling flammable hydrocarbons at elevated temperatures. Safety is not a poster on the wall — it is an engineering and procedural requirement at every level.

Our safety framework covers:

  • Process Hazard Analysis (PHA) for all operating units
  • Mandatory safety training for all plant personnel and contractors
  • Periodic safety audits and mock emergency drills
  • Fire protection systems and gas detection infrastructure
  • Compliance with Maharashtra Pollution Control Board (MPCB) norms

Our operations are regularly inspected by regulatory authorities. We maintain full documentation of effluent treatment, emission monitoring, and waste disposal as required by CPCB and MPCB.

Waste & Emissions

Minimizing What Leaves the Gate

Our approach to waste begins with process design. By maximizing conversion rates and capturing byproducts as saleable material, we reduce the volume of waste that requires treatment or disposal.

Key measures include:

Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP)

On-site ETP treats process wastewater to meet MPCB discharge norms before release. Monitoring is continuous and documented.

Stack Emission Control

Electrostatic precipitators and scrubbing systems on process stacks reduce particulate and gaseous emissions below regulatory limits.

Solid Waste Handling

Spent catalyst and other process residues are handled through authorized disposal channels in compliance with hazardous waste regulations.

Water Management

Cooling water is recirculated. Process water consumption per tonne of PA produced is tracked and optimized as part of ongoing efficiency programs.

Quality Assurance

Consistent Product, Controlled Process

IGPL's in-house laboratory tests every batch of Phthalic Anhydride, Maleic Anhydride, Benzoic Acid, and Diethyl Phthalate before dispatch. Our quality parameters — including purity, colour, and moisture content — are maintained to meet the specifications demanded by downstream resin, plasticizer, and coatings manufacturers.

ISO 9001:2015 certification underpins our quality management system. This is not a badge we display — it is the framework our laboratory and production teams work within every day.

Transparency

Reporting & Disclosure

IGPL is a publicly listed company on NSE and BSE. Our environmental, safety, and governance performance is disclosed as part of our Annual Report in accordance with SEBI Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements (LODR).

As a listed entity, IGPL reports under the Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting (BRSR) framework mandated by SEBI. This includes disclosures on energy consumption, emissions, water usage, waste management, safety incidents, and community engagement.

Our operations are regularly inspected by the Maharashtra Pollution Control Board (MPCB) and comply with all Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) norms for air emissions, effluent discharge, and hazardous waste handling.
