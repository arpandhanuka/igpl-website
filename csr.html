Our Roots

Our Neighbours Are Our Community

IGPL has operated at Taloja, in Raigad District, since 1992. The villages, schools, and families around our plant are not abstract stakeholders — they are the community our employees live in, the children our colleagues' children go to school with.

Our CSR work is focused on the communities in and around Taloja, Panvel, and the broader Raigad district. We believe that a company's responsibility to its neighbourhood is measured in sustained, practical contributions — not one-time events.

"The Company is committed to contribute towards the cause of the Society by way of skill development, education, environment protection and such other related causes."

IGPL CSR Policy Statement

Where We Work

CSR Focus Areas

Education & Literacy

Supporting schools in Taloja and surrounding villages through infrastructure improvements, learning materials, and scholarship programmes for students from economically weaker families.

Skill Development

Vocational training and technical skill-building programmes for youth in Raigad District, helping create employment-ready individuals for the industrial corridor around Taloja.

Environment Protection

Tree plantation drives, community cleanliness initiatives, and awareness programmes on waste segregation and environmental conservation in partnership with local governing bodies.

Community Health

Health check-up camps, blood donation drives, and awareness sessions on hygiene and preventive healthcare conducted in villages around the Taloja industrial area.

Rural Development

Support for local infrastructure needs — drinking water facilities, sanitation, and community spaces — in the panchayat areas neighbouring our operations.

Employee Participation

IGPL employees volunteer in CSR activities — from teaching in local schools to participating in tree plantation and health camps. Our people are the bridge between our company and our community.

Our Approach

Values Measured in Action

CSR at IGPL is not about branding or compliance checklists. It is about the belief that a chemical manufacturing company, operating in a specific place for over three decades, has a genuine obligation to contribute to the wellbeing of the people and environment around it.

We allocate CSR funds in accordance with Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, and our CSR committee — comprising members of our board — oversees programme selection, implementation, and impact assessment.
