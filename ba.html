Overview

From Process Stream to Premium Grade

Benzoic acid (BA) is produced at IGPL as a coproduct of the phthalic anhydride manufacturing process. During the partial oxidation of o-xylene, benzoic acid forms in the process gas and is extracted and purified through a dedicated BA recovery system at the Taloja campus.

IGPL's BA meets or exceeds commercial grade specifications across purity, colour, and trace element requirements. It is supplied as white crystalline flakes and is used in the production of sodium benzoate (food preservative), benzoyl chloride, and as a chemical intermediate in pharmaceutical and specialty synthesis.

As a coproduct tightly linked to PA production volumes, IGPL's BA supply is disciplined and predictable — making it a reliable source for formulators and chemical manufacturers who require consistent quality and scheduling.

Applications

Where Benzoic Acid Is Used

Sodium Benzoate

The largest single use. Conversion to sodium benzoate for use as a food and beverage preservative (E211). Widely used in soft drinks, fruit juices, and condiments.

Benzoyl Chloride

Key chemical intermediate used in the synthesis of benzaldehyde, dyes, perfumes, and pharmaceutical compounds.

Benzaldehyde

Via decarbonylation or Cannizzaro reaction — used in flavours, fragrances, and fine chemical synthesis for food and cosmetic industries.

Phenol Synthesis

Decarboxylation route to phenol — an alternative pathway for specialty phenol production used in resins and polymer manufacture.

Plasticizers

Benzoate esters used as plasticizers and coalescing agents for paints, coatings, and flexible polymer formulations.

Pharmaceutical Intermediates

Used in the synthesis of benzocaine, phenobarbital, and other active pharmaceutical ingredients requiring high-purity aromatic acid precursors.

Production

Recovered from the PA Oxidation Process

The catalytic vapour-phase oxidation of o-xylene to phthalic anhydride produces benzoic acid as a process byproduct. At IGPL, one dedicated BA recovery system captures, purifies, and grades this material to commercial specification.

The BA recovery unit separates the benzoic acid fraction from the PA process condensate, applying fractional distillation and crystallisation steps to achieve the consistent purity and low colour specifications required for food and pharmaceutical downstream uses.

Each production batch is tested by the QC laboratory before release, with full traceability from plant to dispatch. A Certificate of Analysis (CoA) is provided with every shipment.
