Who We Are

India's Chemical Backbone

IG Petrochemicals Ltd. operates five phthalic anhydride production plants at a single integrated campus in Taloja, Maharashtra — 270,000 MTPA of installed capacity and approximately 50% of India's domestic PA capacity, making it a significant supplier to the downstream chemical value chain.

Our core product, phthalic anhydride, is an essential industrial intermediate used in the manufacture of plasticisers, alkyd resins, unsaturated polyester resins, dyes, and pigments. Every significant Indian manufacturer in paints, PVC, and construction chemicals depends on a reliable PA supply chain — and IGPL sits at the centre of it.

Beyond PA, we recover maleic anhydride and benzoic acid as byproducts and produce diethyl phthalate as a downstream value-added product — capturing more of the value chain from the same ortho-xylene feedstock.

Our Story

Strategic Relevance

Why IGPL Matters

IGPL occupies a structural position in India's chemical value chain. Large-scale domestic PA manufacturing is freight-sensitive, quality-critical, and consumed by industries that demand uninterrupted supply — characteristics that favour established producers with scale and continuity.

Scale and Market Position

Phthalic anhydride is a critical industrial intermediate used in plasticisers, coatings, resins, and specialty chemicals. IGPL holds approximately 50% of India's domestic PA capacity — one of India's principal domestic suppliers in this category.

Integrated Value Recovery

A single feedstock — ortho-xylene — produces four commercial products through integrated operations. Maleic anhydride, benzoic acid, and diethyl phthalate are recovered as byproducts, maximising yield from every tonne of raw material.

Structural Logistics Advantage

Located at Taloja, Maharashtra — 50 km from JNPT, India's largest container port. Proximity to port, domestic consumption centres, and raw material sources creates a durable cost and delivery advantage.

Manufacturing

Industrial Scale at a Single Site

Five phthalic anhydride production plants operate on a single integrated campus at Taloja, Maharashtra. Each plant uses the Wacker Chemie Von-Heyden catalytic oxidation process, converting ortho-xylene feedstock into high-purity PA at a combined capacity of 270,000 MTPA.

The Taloja campus sits within India's western chemical corridor, less than 50 km from JNPT — providing direct access to port infrastructure for raw material imports and product exports. Shared utilities, centralised DCS control rooms, and common logistics infrastructure deliver operational efficiency that distributed operations cannot match.
